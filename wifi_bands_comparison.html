🏠 Dual-Band — Best For

✅ Ideal Scenarios

  • Small apartments (up to 100 m²)
  • Families of 2–4 people
  • Up to ~15 connected devices
  • Browsing, email, HD streaming
  • Limited budget

💰 Cost & Models

  • Price range: $50 – $150
  • TP-Link Archer AX55 (Wi-Fi 6)
  • ASUS RT-AX58U (Wi-Fi 6)
  • Fritz!Box 7530 AX
  • Plug & play setup

⚡ Performance

  • 1–2 simultaneous 4K streams
  • Casual gaming — smooth
  • Zoom/Teams — reliable
  • May slow down under heavy load

⚠️ Limitations

  • Congestion with 15+ devices
  • Less future-proof
  • Single 5 GHz channel shared
  • Not ideal for smart home hubs
🚀 Tri-Band — Best For

✅ Ideal Scenarios

  • Large homes (150+ m²)
  • Families of 4–6+ people
  • 20+ connected devices
  • Gamers & 4K streamers
  • Smart homes with many IoT devices

💰 Cost & Models

  • Price range: $150 – $500+
  • TP-Link Archer AX95 (Wi-Fi 6)
  • ASUS RT-AX86U Pro + AiMesh
  • Netgear Nighthawk RAXE300 (6E)
  • Wi-Fi 6E/7 models available

⚡ Performance

  • 3–5+ simultaneous 4K streams
  • Competitive gaming — low ping
  • Separate backhaul for mesh nodes
  • Traffic distributed across bands

⚠️ Considerations

  • Higher upfront cost
  • Slight setup complexity
  • Overkill for basic use
  • Requires compatible devices for 6E
📡 Frequency Bands — Speed vs Range
2.4 GHz
Range ✔✔✔
Wide rangeAll devicesSlower
2.4 GHz — The Long-Distance Band
Best range and wall penetration. Slower speeds but works with every device. Ideal for: smart home sensors, IoT gadgets, devices far from the router. Both Dual-Band and Tri-Band routers include this band.
5 GHz
Speed ✔✔✔✔
Fast speedLess interferenceShorter range
5 GHz — The Speed Band
Much faster than 2.4 GHz with less interference from neighbors. Ideal for: HD/4K streaming, gaming, video calls. Range is shorter — works best in the same or adjacent room. Dual-Band routers have one 5 GHz channel; Tri-Band routers have two.
6 GHz
Ultra speed ✔✔✔✔✔
Ultra fastNo congestionWi-Fi 6E/7 only
6 GHz — The Future Band (Wi-Fi 6E / Wi-Fi 7)
The cleanest and fastest band available. Zero congestion from older devices. Perfect for: cloud gaming, 8K streaming, VR/AR. Short range — works best close to the router. Requires both router AND device to support Wi-Fi 6E or Wi-Fi 7.
